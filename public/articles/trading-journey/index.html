My Trading Journey

Posted by: Seth Lakowske

Published: 2021-06-1

This is a story about the lessons learned as I began intraday trading.

Lessons learned

  • Decision Fatigue and over trading:
    Humans lose their ability to make good decisions as the number of decisions increases throughout a day. Trading involves making decisions such as whether it's a good situation to buy or sell. So the more trading decisions that are made per day, the lower quality the trading decisions become. As fatigue sets in, trades become more inconsistent and sloppy.
  • Greed:
    When a trader starts out profitable enough on the day to stop trading, emotions like greed tempt traders to continue trading. The naive idea being that the trading is profitable and so the more trading, the more profits will bemade. This is incorrect due to decision fatigue explained above. Quality trading decisions are finite and lower the quality decisions made later in the day almostalway results in less profit.
  • Emotions whil trading:
    Real trading involves the gain and loss of meaningful amounts of money. When a trade is placed and meaningful amounts of money can be lost, traders can be susceptible to swings in emotion.
