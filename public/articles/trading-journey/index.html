My Trading Journey

Posted by: Seth Lakowske

Published: 2021-06-1

This is a story about the lessons learned as I began intraday trading.

Lessons learned

  • Decision Fatigue and over trading:
    Humans lose their ability to make good decisions as the number of decisions increases throughout a day due to mental fatigue. Trading involves making decisions such as whether a good setup to buy or sell is present. As the number of trading decisions made per day increases, fatigue sets in, trades tend to become more inconsistent and sloppy.
  • Greed:
    When a trader starts out profitable enough on the day to stop trading, emotions like greed tempt traders to continue trading. The naive idea being that the trading is profitable and so the more trading, the more profits will be made. This is incorrect due to decision fatigue explained above. Quality trading decisions are finite and low quality decisions made later in the day tend to result in less profit.
  • Emotions while trading:
    Real trading involves the gain and loss of meaningful amounts of money. When a trade is placed and meaningful amounts of money can be gained or lost, traders can be susceptible to swings in emotion. Swings in emotions tend to create biases unrelated to the underlying information in the market.
    The risk with gains is an emotional bias of overconfidence with the implied assumption that the good market conditions will continue. This can result in overbetting just when good conditions are plateuing and starting to recede.
